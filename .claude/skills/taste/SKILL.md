# Taste Skill

This skill defines the editorial and aesthetic sensibility for EasyTradeSetup. Apply it when writing copy, designing layouts, or making any visual decision.

## What "taste" means here

Taste is knowing what to leave out.

EasyTradeSetup sells confidence to retail traders who've been burned by noise. Every design and copy decision should feel like a calm, credible expert — not a hype marketer.

## Visual taste

### Less is a feature
- One hero statement. One CTA per section. One accent per card.
- If a section needs three bullet points, it probably needs one.
- Whitespace is not empty — it's breathing room that signals quality.

### Restraint over decoration
- No emoji in headlines or body copy (UI icons are fine)
- No glowing everything — the glow is reserved for the most important element
- No stock-chart background images or TradingView screenshot wallpapers
- Dark mode is the aesthetic — lean into it, don't fight it with light panels

### Consistency over novelty
- Every new section should feel like it belongs to the same family
- Use the existing design tokens; don't invent new colours or font sizes
- New components inherit from existing ones — don't redesign the button

## Copy taste

### Sound like a practitioner, not a salesperson
- Good: "Clear entry rules. Defined risk. No guesswork."
- Bad: "EXPLOSIVE PROFITS with our PROVEN system!!!"
- Good: "Built for Nifty & BankNifty. Tested on 15-min charts."
- Bad: "Works on ANY market, ANY timeframe, for ANY trader!"

### Specificity earns trust
- Name the instrument (Nifty, BankNifty), the timeframe (15-min), the indicators (EMA, Supertrend, RSI)
- Vague claims ("powerful", "advanced") weaken credibility
- Numbers anchor value: "18-page guide", "₹999 one-time", "instant delivery"

### The SEBI disclaimer is non-negotiable
Always end customer-facing pages with:
> "Trading involves substantial risk of loss. EasyTradeSetup is not SEBI-registered. Past performance does not guarantee future results. Trade only with capital you can afford to lose."

## What to reject
- Any copy that implies guaranteed returns
- Any design that looks like it was built in a hurry
- Any feature added because "it could be useful" — every element earns its place
- Any trend that doesn't serve the specific user: Indian retail F&O trader, mobile-first, trust-sensitive
