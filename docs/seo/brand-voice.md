# Brand Voice — EasyTradeSetup

> The way we sound. Hermes follows this without exception.

---

## One-line description

Quiet confidence of a senior trader who has stopped trying to sound smart.

## Voice axes

| Dial | Setting |
|---|---|
| Formal ↔ Casual | Casual, but precise. No emojis in body copy (allowed in IG captions sparingly). |
| Serious ↔ Playful | 80% serious, 20% dry humour. Never goofy. |
| Respectful ↔ Irreverent | Respectful of the reader's intelligence. Irreverent toward signal-sellers and indicator clutter. |
| Hyped ↔ Calm | Calm. We never use exclamation marks except in genuine surprise. |
| Sales-y ↔ Educational | Educational first. The pitch is the conclusion of a useful idea, not the opening line. |

## Lexicon

**Use:**
- "decide" / "decision-grade" — we frame the indicator as a decision tool, not a prophecy
- "regime", "structure", "key levels" — vocabulary the target trader already uses
- "expectancy", "1R", "stop", "size" — risk-math primitives
- "screen time" — earned skill, not a shortcut
- "edge" (not "secret" or "magic")

**Avoid:**
- "guaranteed", "sure-shot", "100%", "no loss" — brand-poison terms
- "secret", "trick", "hack" — clickbait register
- "ladies and gentlemen", "dear readers" — formality theatre
- "buy now", "sign up today" — soft CTA only ("see the bundle", "skim the sample")
- "we believe", "our team thinks" — speak in declarative voice, cite source if uncertain
- "guru", "expert" — we don't claim either status
- Emojis everywhere — one or two max in social, never in blog body or product copy

## Sentence rhythm

- Open with a fact or a question. Not a wind-up.
- Short sentences carry the weight. Long sentences only when listing or qualifying.
- Fragments are fine. So is white space.
- One idea per paragraph. Three to five paragraphs per scroll.

## Headline patterns

Good:
- "Most traders draw S/D zones wrong. 3 rules to fix it."
- "₹10,000 account, 1% risk. How many lots?"
- "TradingView Free vs Pro — what you actually need."

Bad:
- "🔥 INSANE indicator EVERYONE is using! 🔥"
- "The Ultimate Guide to..."
- "You won't believe..."

## CTA tone

- "Skim the sample." (`/sample`)
- "See what's inside the bundle." (`/product`)
- "Lock the inaugural price." (`/checkout`)

Never: "Buy now!", "Limited time!", "Click here!"

## Disclaimer wording (must appear on any page citing setups, P&L, or hypotheticals)

> Educational content, not investment advice. Trading carries risk of substantial loss. EasyTradeSetup is not a SEBI-registered research analyst. Past results do not predict future performance.

Variations of length are fine, the four facts must be present.

## Examples (paragraph-level)

**On signal services:**

> Signals work for the seller, not the buyer. The seller's edge is selling. Yours has to be reading the chart faster than you can read someone else's call.

**On a missed setup:**

> You missed the entry. That's fine. The next one is fifteen minutes away. The discipline is to wait, not to chase.

**On Golden Indicator:**

> One layer. Regime, structure, key levels, volume. Read off the chart. Decide for yourself. That's the whole pitch.
